Mission Statement

The Eating Disorder Outreach Program (EDOP) serves as a space for people with or without lived experience to find support and community. Through organized meetings and events, our group will serve as a voice of inspiration and a source of support for students in this community. We are a non-profit organization dedicated to honest and open conversation surrounding eating disorder education, reform, and advocacy. We hope to inform others about the realities of eating disorders - especially those that are misrepresented or completely excluded from traditional media through educational workshops and lectures. We intend to educate our peers about diet culture as a form of oppression and raise overall awareness about eating disorders. We also provide resources for those recovering from eating disorders and those supporting friends or family with lived experience. We plan to establish a support group for those with lived experience to find comfort and assist each other on their way to recovery. EDOP recognizes that fatphobia, body shaming, and all associated forms of diet culture are forms of oppression that derive from the patriarchy, misogyny, colonialism, white supremacy, heteronormativity, etc. and seek to silence marginalized voices. We recognize that certain bodies are marginalized in society and that thin privilege is the norm. We believe that everybody has a fundamental right to access help and treatment. In EDOP, people will find that they are not alone, they will have a safe outlet to express everything they’re experiencing - from stigma, shame and fear, and in time, recovery.

About Us

Membership

Eligibility to Club X is open to all full-time undergraduate members of the Brown community and does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, age, national or ethnic origin, disability, status as a veteran, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, or sex. Majority Membership consists of full-time undergraduate students.

Officers/Leadership

The leadership of Project LETS shall be a President. The term of office shall be one year, beginning at the first meeting of each academic year.

Co-Chairs/Leadership

The Co-Chairs shall be responsible for organizing and conducting meetings of the members and for coordinating group business, as well as maintaining the finances of the group and communicating with the Undergraduate Finance Board regarding group expenditures. Co-Chairs are responsible for making sure everything is running smoothly and every team member is aware of what everyone else is doing. They are responsible for frequently checking in with each team member, maintaining a running knowledge of everything that is going on in the organization, and ensuring that no one member is overburdened. Additionally, Co-chairs are responsible for prioritizing initiatives, meeting with administrators and other student organizations, and running meetings.

Event Coordinator/Business Manager

The Event Coordinator shall make arrangements for EDOP projects including weekly meetings, support group meetings, outreach, fundraising, and special events (such as info sessions).  They will work closely with the Co-Leaders and Treasurer, along with the rest of the group, to establish goals and budgets for each project. Along with the Social Media Coordinator, they will oversee publicity arrangements, including flyer distribution and online announcements, either by managing these themself or delegating tasks to members of the group. During events, the Event Coordinator will provide direction to group members, attendees, and site managers as needed, making themself available for any problems that might arise.

Secretary

The duties of the Secretary shall include maintaining all the written records of the Club and particularly the minutes of all regular and executive meetings. The minutes shall be made available to the general membership via electronic mail, or alternative means. The secretary shall also be responsible for communication and correspondence with other individuals and groups as directed by the executive team. The secretary shall also be responsible for reserving rooms for meetings and events.

Treasurer/Leadership

The duties of the Treasurer shall include receiving all Club funds, keeping full and accurate account of these funds and depositing all money into the clubs given student account. The Treasurer shall also interact with Student Activities personnel on behalf of the other members for any funding/budgeting purposes. Fund disbursement may not be made except by approval of the President or by a majority vote. The Club’s account shall be current at all times and a full financial report shall be available upon demand of any club member or advisor. The Treasurer shall be required to present the account balance and all transactions at each general meeting. The Treasurer shall arrange transportation to all Club functions that require it. Lastly, the Treasurer shall assist in planning/organizing/budgeting for any events and related club expenses/fundraisers.

Webmaster/Leadership

Response for the assembly and upkeep of an anonymous website/texting service for assistance or advice. They shall make the website through computer programing and repurpose the site for the sake of our specific needs. The webmasters will make sure that it is a safe space and regulate the content in both posts and responses. They will keep up to date with the latest submissions so they can get their submission posted as soon as possible. Lastly, they will go through comments to make sure they are appropriate.

Social Media Coordinator/Leadership

The Social Media Coordinator will establish and run our group’s Instagram account for body positivity and publicizing our events. They will monitor the account to ensure that triggering or offense comments are not posted. They will post about our events on the Project LETS private and public Facebook pages. They will collaborate with the webmaster, Event Planner, and Co-Chairs to maintain a unified message about our events and plans. The Social Media Coordinator will regularly post on the group’s Instagram and organize the making and printing of an article in the Brown Daily Herald with the Event Planner. They will find and take opportunities to spread the word about the group around campus and create Facebook events for our group events.

Elections

Officers shall be elected during the latter part of the second semester of each academic year. To be elected, a candidate must receive a majority of votes cast based off of their submitted application for the position. If no candidate receives a majority of votes on the first ballot, runoffs shall be conducted between the top two vote-getters. There shall be no limit on the number of terms an officer can fill.

Amendment Procedure

An amendment shall be brought before the group by a majority vote. The President shall notify all current members of the group that an amendment to the group’s constitution is considered. The amendment will then be considered at the meeting following its announcement to the group. To be accepted, an amendment must gain support from two thirds of the group membership. Once an amendment has been accepted by EDOP, it shall be given to the UCS Coordinator of Student Activities at least two full days before the next regularly scheduled full council meeting. Upon receipt of a simple majority from UCS, the amendment shall be considered a formal part of EDOP’s constitution.
